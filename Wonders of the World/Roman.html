Key Highlights

Location

Rome is the capital of Italy and home to the Colosseum, located in the heart of the city.

Construction

The Colosseum was constructed between 70-80 AD under the emperors Vespasian and Titus.

Significance

The Colosseum is an iconic symbol of ancient Rome, known for gladiatorial games and Roman architecture.

Rome Facts

Rome is an ancient city with a rich history, renowned for its monuments, architecture, and art.

The Colosseum could hold up to 50,000 spectators and is one of the greatest works of Roman engineering.

It is considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
